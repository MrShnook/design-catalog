In the year 2250, humanity had reached new heights in technological advancements. Colonizing other planets had become a norm, and intergalactic travel was commonplace. People could teleport from one planet to another in mere seconds, and flying cars filled the skies. Artificial intelligence was integrated into every aspect of life, from managing households to running complex interplanetary businesses. However, despite all the progress, there were still issues that needed to be addressed. Climate change had taken a toll on many planets, and resources were becoming scarce. The government had implemented strict regulations on resource usage and waste management to combat the problem. In this world, two friends, Ava from Earth and Kael from Mars, met during their studies at a prestigious intergalactic university. Despite coming from vastly different cultures and backgrounds, they quickly bonded over their mutual love of adventure and their thirst for knowledge. They spent many years traveling and exploring the universe together, facing challenges and overcoming obstacles. They became known as the dynamic duo, and their adventures were the stuff of legend. One day, they received a distress signal from a remote planet on the outskirts of the galaxy. The inhabitants of the planet were in dire need of help, as their entire civilization was threatened by a mysterious force. Without hesitation, Ava and Kael set out on a dangerous journey to the unknown planet, determined to save its people and uncover the truth behind the mysterious force. Their journey would be filled with challenges, but their friendship and determination would see them through. They were determined to make a difference and leave their mark on the universe, just like the great explorers and pioneers of the past.

### Story

Chubbo is a family of expressive slab serif fonts. It can lend a unique voice to the larger-sized texts in posters, brochures, website headlines and apps. A lot of Chubbo’s expressiveness and friendly-nature comes from the forms of its slab serifs themselves. These all end in rounded terminals, giving the typeface a soft feeling. The family encompasses five weights ranging in stroke thickness from ExtraLight to Bold. Each Chubbo weight has a complementary italic, too. Chubbo’s italics are even livelier than its upright fonts. The fonts’ characters are not sloped versions of the upright forms. Instead, they are redrawn in a more cursive style. Inside the italics, the stroke of the pen is kept. They are the most exciting contribution type designer Rafał Buchner brought to the project. Aside from Chubbo’s availability as ten ‘classic’ fonts, the design also ships as two Variable Fonts. There is one Variable Font for Chubbo’s upright styles and another for its italics.

In each style, the fonts’ strokes are optically monolinear. Some letters in the italic styles’ lowercase prove the exception, to strengthen their script-like appearance. Chubbo’s upright fonts have double-storey ‘a’ and ‘g’ forms per default. A single-storey ‘g’ is available as an alternate. In the italics, the ‘a’ and ‘g’ are always single-storey. Through an Opentype feature, you can replace the default forms of Chubbo’s ‘g’ and ‘y’ with alternates. The descenders of the standard italic ‘g’ and ‘y’ have closed loops; alternate glyphs provide open forms for those two letters. In both the upright and italic styles, the default ‘G’ has a curled beard that descends below the baseline, but there is a version with a straight, non-descending beard available as an alternate. All Chubbo fonts also have an alternate dollar sign where the stroke does not bisect the entire S-form.

With more than 390 glyphs per font, Chubbo supports all of the characters needed for European languages written with the Latin script. Chubbo’s lowercase letters have a moderate x-height, but their ascenders rise above the tops of the fonts’ capitals. The fonts feature interior forms that are clear and legible, no matter their total size. The typeface will lend a lively voice to any situation it is applied in.
